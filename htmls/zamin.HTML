Geothermal energy

We all know that the center of the earth has an extremely high temperature and as we move away from it, this temperature decreases. Geothermal energy, as its name suggests, is energy that uses the heat of the lower layers of the earth to produce electricity. To produce this energy, they make a big hole near volcanoes (because near volcanoes, magma is near the upper layers of the earth). They dig another hole next to the bigger one so that they are connected at the end. Then they pour a lot of cold water into the big hole. The cold water is turned into heat by the heat of the lower layer of the earth and comes to the surface of the earth through the side cavity and in the power plant, the steam generated by the water drives the turbines and produces electricity.
